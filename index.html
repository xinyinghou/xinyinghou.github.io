I am currently a PhD student at School of Information - University of Michigan, working with Professor Barbara Ericson. My research focuses on Human-Computer Interaction, Educational Technology, and CS Education.

Previously, I earned my Masters degree in HCI from CMU where my academic advisors were Professor Kenneth R. Koedinger and Professor Bruce M. McLaren. I received my B.A. in Sociology from Nanjing University.

I am interested in integrating learning science theories into human-centered design techniques to engage students and promote learning, with a particular focus on programming learning.
I apply mixed-methods approach and Machine Learning techniques to understand learning behaviors and build new tools in a variety of programming learning environments (online and physical classrooms; formal and informal).
Recently, I am working on two projects:
(1) Develop more scalable, low-cost, easy-to-implement, and high-quality scaffolding tools for novice programmers
(2) Understand and Develop tools to integrate GitHub Copilot into K-12 programming learning
